Projects

In environments with high stellar density, three-body interactions are frequent and can give rise to the dynamical formation of binary BHs (BBHs). These BBHs can then inspiral and merge emitting gravitational waves. If their merger remnant is not ejected from the cluster, it can undergo further three-body encounters, form new BBHs and go through new mergers. This process is called hierarchical mergers and can lead to the formation of remnant BHs with progressively higher and higher mass. In the DEMOBLACK team, we developed a code called FASTCLUSTER which simulates the hierarchical merger phenomenon in different environments: globular, young and nuclear star clusters as well as active galactic nuclei (AGNs). I'm a developer of the FASTCLUSTER branch for the simulation of AGNs.
The AGN environment stands out with respect to other potential hosts of hierarchical BBH mergers because of the presence of a dense gaseous disk which fuels mass accretion onto a central super-massive BH (SMBH). The stellar-sized BHs orbiting the SMBH are thus subject to strong gas torques and change their orbital properties, similarly to what happens to planets in protoplanetary disks. This process generally brings BHs closer to each other and significantly increases the rate of three-body interactions, thus leading to the efficient production of higher-mass BHs.
